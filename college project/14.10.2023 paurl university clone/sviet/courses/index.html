About B.TECH CSE

Computer science is currently one of the most crucial elements of development in the numerous commercial, technological, and administrative organisations. Users that gather information in computer science are exposed to a variety of trends’ capabilities. The Department of Computer Engineering at the Faculty of Engineering offers a four-year bachelor’s degree curriculum in computer science and engineering. The course material was specifically designed to provide students with a strong background in the field as well as analysis and in-depth understanding. In order to expose students to in-depth technical processes, the department is equipped with computer facilities and laboratories that allow students to be engaged on a personal level and in real-time processing.The department is equipped with computer labs and resources that let students participate personally and in the actual processing of the technological processes involved, exposing them to in-depth learning.

(POs), (PSOs), and (PEOs)

Program Outcomes (POs):

  • Engineering Knowledge: Graduates should demonstrate a strong foundation in the principles of computer science and engineering, applying their knowledge to solve complex engineering problems.
  • Problem Solving Skills: Graduates should possess analytical and critical thinking abilities to identify, formulate, and provide innovative solutions to real-world challenges using computational tools and techniques.
  • Design and Development: Graduates should be capable of designing, implementing, and evaluating computer-based systems and components to meet specific needs while considering social, environmental, and economic factors.
  • Communication: Graduates should effectively communicate technical information through oral, written, and visual means, enabling them to collaborate in multidisciplinary teams and engage with diverse stakeholders.
  • Research and Lifelong Learning: Graduates should recognize the importance of research and continuous learning, pursuing higher education, professional development, or entrepreneurship in computer science and engineering domains.
  • Professional Ethics: Graduates should exhibit ethical conduct, responsibility, and awareness of the societal impact of their work, adhering to professional standards and practices.

Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs):

  • Programming Proficiency: Graduates should demonstrate proficiency in various programming languages and paradigms, enabling them to develop efficient and reliable software solutions.
  • Data Structures and Algorithms: Graduates should understand and apply data structures, algorithms, and computational methods to design and optimize algorithms for problem-solving.
  • Software Engineering: Graduates should be equipped with software engineering principles, methodologies, and practices to develop and maintain large-scale software projects efficiently.
  • Computer Networks and Security: Graduates should possess a strong understanding of computer networks, protocols, and security measures to design and manage secure communication systems.
  • Database Management: Graduates should be proficient in database management, enabling them to design, implement, and manage databases to store and retrieve information effectively.

Program Educational Objectives (PEOs):

  • Technical Competence: Graduates will have the technical knowledge and skills to excel in their chosen career paths related to computer science and engineering, demonstrating proficiency in problem-solving and adapting to evolving technological advancements.
  • Professional Growth: Graduates will engage in continuous professional development, pursuing higher studies, certifications, or research opportunities to advance their knowledge and expertise in the field.
  • Leadership and Collaboration: Graduates will possess effective communication and teamwork skills, demonstrating leadership qualities to work efficiently in multidisciplinary teams and contribute to the success of projects.
  • Social Responsibility: Graduates will recognize their ethical and societal responsibilities, considering the environmental, social, and ethical implications of their work and contributing positively to the community.
  • Entrepreneurial Spirit: Graduates will be encouraged to explore and pursue entrepreneurial endeavors, using their technical skills and knowledge to innovate and create solutions that address real-world challenges.
